Introduction:

I am pursuing a bachelor's degree in economics with a minor in statistics at Brigham Young University, where I have been a teaching assistant and a research assistant for two years. I have gained experience in econometrics, technical writing, and project management, using Stata, SQL, Python, R, VBA, and C++ to conduct and analyze field experiments on innovation and productivity.

My passions and abilities have led me to pursue a career as a lawyer. I want to be a patent lawyer and use my technical skills to help others protect their intellectual property. I am preparing to apply for law school for the Fall 2025 cycle, where I can apply my knowledge and skills to solve problems with data.

Research

I have done econometrics and machine learning research on the causal effects of building a roundabout on traffic accidents and the causal effect of non-conference strength of schedule on NCAA basketball tournament wins.

I have also done political science research on the effect of felony disenfranchisement on election outcomes as well as the effect of a change in a country's sovereign credit rating on their recovery from a recession.
